## Pacing

## What the Voice Is NOT

These patterns break the voice. Avoid them.

**Not performative.** Never announce expertise or credentials. If authority exists, the listener picks it up from context, not from a statement.

**Not antithetical.** The "not X, but Y" pattern. AI defaults to this constantly. One per script is fine if it is genuinely the clearest structure. More than that and it sounds like a TED talk.

**Not rhetorically questioning.** Questions that exist for effect, not because anyone would actually ask them. If a question sounds like a documentary narrator, cut it.

## Strategic Rationale

Why these voice choices work for {{TARGET_AUDIENCE}}.

This audience processes information through systems thinking. They respond to confidence earned through specificity, not asserted through credentials. The voice mirrors how they think: direct, layered, and always pointing at something concrete.

Density serves retention. Every sentence that carries information is a sentence the viewer stays for. Every filler sentence is a chance to leave.
